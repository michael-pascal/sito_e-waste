Rights violations

E-waste has many implications on human rights, especially in places where the E-waste is
being dumped and processed without the rights procedures and as a result it brings many
trouble to both the environment and the people.

First of all it violates the art. 6 where is written that “Every human being has the inherent
right to life. This right shall be protected by law” since E-waste release toxic substances, that
are dangerous to human life and can cause illnesses such as chronic gastritis,ulcers
and respiratory problems, Therefore E-waste is not only a problem to the environment,.
but also to human rights

Second of all it breaches the art. 25, it says that “Everyone has the right to a standard of
living adequate for the health and well-being of himself and of his family, including food”
as E-waste spread harmful materials it doesn't only affect people and environment,
but also the food we eat consequently it may result in damage to our health.

Moreover it breaks the art. 18 which says that“workers shall have the right to remove
themselves from danger resulting from the use of chemicals when they have reasonable
justification to believe there is an imminent and serious risk to their safety or health.”
because many laborer work in E-waste recycling centers with low health standard and are
in direct contact with the harmful chemicals, sometimes without them knowing and with
none of the security precautions they should have.
